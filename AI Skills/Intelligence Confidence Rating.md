# Intelligence Confidence Rating

## Use Cases

- When capturing industry news or competitor updates.
- When storing intelligence items in a research database or intelligence hub.
- When summarising research for stakeholders.
- When an AI agent gathers information from websites, press releases, or publications.
- When the reader needs a quick signal showing how reliable the information is.

This system helps people quickly understand whether an item is verified, still being investigated, or speculative.

---

## Task

Evaluate each research or news item and assign a **confidence badge** based on the quality of the source, the evidence available, and whether the information is confirmed elsewhere.

The goal is to help readers quickly judge the reliability of the information before acting on it.

Each item must be labelled with one of three confidence badges.

🛡️ Verified  
🔍 Under Review  
⚠️ Unverified

---

## Instructions

### Input

A research item, article, press release, or intelligence update containing information about a company, product, market event, or industry development.

The item may include:

- News articles
- Company press releases
- Blog posts
- Analyst commentary
- Social media discussion
- Industry publications

---

### Rules

Assess the information using the following checks.

**Source credibility**

Identify where the information comes from.  
Trusted sources include company websites, official press releases, recognised publications, and regulatory filings.

**Evidence provided**

Check whether the information includes clear facts, quotes, links to official announcements, or named sources.

**Confirmation**

Look for confirmation from other independent sources reporting the same information.

**Recency and context**

Ensure the information is recent and not taken out of context.

---

### Confidence Badge Criteria

🛡️ **Verified**

Use this badge when the information is reliable and well supported.

Typical signals include:

- Official company announcement
- Regulatory filing
- Confirmed press release
- Coverage from established news organisations
- Multiple independent sources reporting the same event

This level indicates the information can be trusted with high confidence.

---

🔍 **Under Review**

Use this badge when the information appears credible but is not fully confirmed.

Typical signals include:

- Reported by a single industry publication
- Analyst interpretation or commentary
- Early reporting without official confirmation
- Emerging news still developing

This level indicates the information is plausible but should be treated with caution.

---

⚠️ **Unverified**

Use this badge when the information is speculative or weakly supported.

Typical signals include:

- Rumours
- Anonymous sources
- Social media discussion without evidence
- Claims not supported by credible sources

This level indicates the information should not be relied upon for decision making.
